24.A Political Fresh Start

Immediately after the liberation of Wolfenbüttel from National Socialism on April 11, 1945, by American forces, City Commander Captain Manning laid the foundation for a political new beginning based on Western democratic principles. On April 18, 1945, he appointed former Social Democratic district councilor Willy Mull as the first post-war mayor of Wolfenbüttel. Willy Mull had been a victim of National Socialism. In 1933, he was dismissed from his position as a senior district secretary due to his involvement in local politics with the SPD. After the assassination attempt on Hitler on July 20, 1944, Mull was arrested by the Gestapo during the nationwide "Thunderstorm" roundup and imprisoned in the notorious "Camp 21" in Hallendorf. On April 1, 1946, the British military government introduced the framework for a municipal constitution in their occupation zone, modeled after the British system. This system established a dual leadership structure with Willy Mull as the full-time city director and Otto Rüdiger as the honorary political mayor. The first free municipal election of the post-war period was held on September 15, 1946.
